Return to the ocean. Subnautica 2 is the sequel to the acclaimed survival game where you must explore a mysterious alien ocean, discover new creatures, build bases, and uncover the secrets of a lost civilization. The game features an improved biomod system, new vehicles, and a richer progression system than its predecessor.

Hidden Stuff Most Players Miss

🌊 Ocean Currents Are Highways

Most players think currents only drag you into danger. Actually, some currents connect biomes and can massively speed up travel if you learn their routes. Instead of fighting them, ride them.

🔍 Scan EVERYTHING

The blueprint system is mostly gone. Scanning creatures, alien structures, and biomes gives Adaptation Points for Biomods. Some players reach advanced abilities way earlier because they scan literally everything.

⬆️ Vertical Exploration Rules

People keep missing caves because they're looking forward instead of up and down. There are hidden oxygen pockets, resource clusters, and entrances above areas you'd normally swim past. Especially around coral structures.

🏗️ Build Away From Predators

The devs are surprised by how many people build bases beside dangerous creatures and then complain about attacks. Creature behavior adjustments are now a roadmap priority because of this.

🔭 The Observatory Secret

After activating the generator, the Observatory opens. You need to swim upward inside the planetarium section. A lot of players completely missed the vertical entrance.

↗️ Diagonal Swimming Is Faster

Diagonal movement gives slightly faster travel and makes hostile creatures less accurate. Could change later since it's Early Access.

💀 Death Is Forgiving

Unlike Subnautica 1, items don't explode everywhere, so dying isn't always the end of the world. The current Early Access is more forgiving overall.

⚠️ Don't Speedrun

Players who rush the story and ignore scanning suddenly miss biomods and upgrades that make deeper zones easier. Exploration progression is WAY more important than speedrunning.

Biomods System

🧬 What Are Biomods?

Adaptation upgrades gained by scanning creatures, structures, and biomes. More important than old blueprint systems.

📊 Adaptation Points

Earned through scanning. Higher priority than speedrunning story. Unlock powerful late-game abilities.

🔧 Current State

Still being tuned heavily in Early Access. Better balancing and more passive slots planned for EA 1.1.

⭐ Future Expansion

Unknown Worlds wants biomods to become one of the biggest progression systems. Late-game characters will feel vastly different.

Pro Tip: Watch the biomod system closely. Most players treat it like old Subnautica upgrades, but it's shaping up to be the core progression mechanic. Characters with different biomod combinations will have completely different playstyles.
